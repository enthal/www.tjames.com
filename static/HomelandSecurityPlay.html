Homeland Security

 

A play by Timothy James

Monday, October 29, 2001

 

Girl:  What?  Who are you?  What are you doing in my room?

Officer ONE:  Officer Denkins, Homeland security.

Officer Two: mmhmm

(pause, they stare at each other)

Girl:  (shouts)  Mom!!

(Officer Two:  goes back to rummaging, pause)

Girl:  Sooo…what are…  hey that’s mine!  Don’t touch that, creep, that’s my journal! (seizes journal from Officer Two)

Officer ONE:  Ma’am, we have authorization to search.

Officer Two: mmhmm

(Officer Two  goes back to rummaging)

Girl:  What?!  Who are you?  What authorization?  Hey that’s my underwear!

Officer ONE:  We have a high-correlative probable terrorist ID on your person.  (rummages through a bunch of papers [use the script!], before settling on one) Amanda.  Amanda Witherspoon.

Girl:    High-corr… huh?

Officer ONE:   You are in fact Amanda Witherspoon, security ID 215-23-0348.

Girl:   (getting scared) Yeh… Yeah..  That’s my social security num…

Officer ONE:   Yes, your security ID number, as registered with Homeland Security.

Officer Two:  (still rummaging) Nothing “social” about it.

Officer ONE:  Your recorded activities correlate highly with the terrorist profile.  Since you have positively assented to your matching security ID, and are interrupting an authorized search of a known or suspected terrorist who is yourself, and are physically present, we’ll be proceeding to sub-sub-operation delta-nine-gamma.

Officer Two: mmhmm

(Officer Two stands up and looks at Girl.  They do not move)

Girl:  I’m not a…I’m not a terrorist.

Officer Two: Sir, I think we may be premature for sub-sub-operation delta-nine-gamma.

Officer ONE:  (pause) Yes.  Yes, I don’t think she fits the criteria.

Girl:  What criteria?

Officer Two: We thought you’d be older.

Officer ONE:  (censoring tone) Denkins.

Officer Two: Sir.

Officer ONE:   Ma’am.  Is it correct that this document is your personal journal? (takes journal)

Girl:  (shouts louder)  MOM!!!!

Officer ONE:   Your mother is not home.

Girl:  What…?  How…?  How do you know that?

(Officer Two:  goes back to rummaging)

Officer ONE:   Our search authorization and search plan documents state that your mother has an appointment during this time period and is thus and therefore not to be on these premises.  Our authorized covert searches as contained under general search plan sub-operation delta-nine are conducted only without searchee knowledge, and that is to say when vehicle or premises of searchee are vacant and unoccupied by serchee, his co-terrorists, or other parties.

Officer Two: mmhmm

Girl:   But… I’m here.  I’m home.  I’m on the premises. 

Officer Two:  The search plan says otherwise.

Officer ONE:  (more censoring tone) Denkins!

Officer Two: Sir!

Girl:  (talking to Officer ONE, points at Officer ONE) Wait… I thought you were Denkins.

Officer Two: He is.

Officer ONE:   I am.

Girl:   (beginning to relax) I… see.

Officer ONE:  But since you, the known or suspected terrorist, have arrived at said premises, we must emergency abort said sub-sub-plan under which we are authorized to carry out said search on said terrorist premises. 

Officer Two: mmhmm

Girl:  But I’m not a terrorist.  I’m…. I’m a seventh grader!

Officer ONE:   That remains to be seen.

Girl:   That I’m a seventh grader?!

Officer ONE:   That you’re a terrorist.

Girl:   I’m not a terrorist.

Officer ONE:  The search authorization document states that you are a known or suspected terrorist.

Girl:   Am not.

Officer Two: Are so.

Girl:   (shouting) Am NOT!!

Officer Two: (shouting) ARE SO!!!

Officer ONE:   (angry now) DENKINS!!

Officer Two:  Sir!  Yes Sir!

Girl:   I’m not a terrorist!

(pause)

Officer ONE:   Ma’am.  If you are not a terrorist than you need not worry.  We are officers of the department of Homeland Security, carrying out an authorized search of a known or suspected terrorist.  If you are not, in fact, a known or suspected terrorist, we will determine that in due course through authorized search-and-surveill operations such as this one.

Officer Two:  If you have nothing to hide…

Officer ONE:   (proudly) Then you have nothing to fear.  Not from us, and, thanks to the department of Homeland Security, not from terrorists. 

Officer Two:  Such as your self.

Officer ONE:   If you are not a terrorist, and that remains to be seen!

Girl:   I see.

Officer ONE:   You will!  This journal!  (opens it)

Girl:  Hey!  Don’t read that!  That’s secret stuff!  That’s private?

Officer ONE:   Secrets?  Terrorist secrets.  So you do have something to hide!

Girl:   Of course, stupid!  That’s my journal!  Nobody can read it but me, especially not boys!  So give it back!

Officer Two:  We can’t do that.

Officer ONE:   I’m afraid we can’t do that.  This document –

Officer Two:  -- Your journal --

Officer ONE:  -- now represents a record of known or suspected terrorist activities of a known or suspected terrorist --

Officer Two: -- That’s you –

Officer ONE:  -- and was discovered during a search of the premises of said terrorist  --

Officer Two: -- that’s your room here --

Officer ONE:  -- which are the premises in which known or suspected terrorist activities are suspected to be carried out --

Officer Two: -- that’s listed in the search authorization  --

Officer ONE:  -- and which furthermore are known or suspected to have served as a base of terrorist operation for terrorist activities or recorded activities that correlate to patterns of terrorism –

Officer Two: -- that’s also listed in the search authorization  --

Officer ONE:  -- carried out by said terrorist --

Officer Two: -- that’s you again --

Officer ONE:  -- and/or his co-terrorists and accomplices  --

Officer Two: -- that’s listed in the search authorization, too  --

Officer ONE:  -- as listed in the search authorization.

(pause)

Girl:   No, dorks, that’s my journal.  That’s where I write about boys who like me, and boys I like, and dorky boys like you, and girls I like, and girls I hate.  I’m not a terrorist.  I don’t correlate with any terrorists, or whatever.  So give it back.  Now. 

Officer ONE:  Actually…  You do correlate with the terrorist profile.  Highly.

Officer Two: mmhmm

Officer ONE:  It’s all right here in the authorization.

Girl:  Like how?

Officer ONE:  (Flips pages before picking one)  According to our terrorist activity statistical correlation systems, and based on the following data collection sets, your correlation is 92.4 percent. 

Officer Two: A dead ringer. 

Officer ONE:  The inner correlative circle.

Officer Two: A red eye. 

Girl:  Huh?

Officer ONE:  Data includes…
- ONE: positive computer facial recognitions from security cameras on busses, stores, and ATMs, marking you as transiting to areas and premises of other highly correlated known or suspected terrorists
- TWO: positive computer facial recognitions of other highly correlated known or suspected terrorists to these premises
- THREE: known membership or association with organizations known to have members who are highly correlated known or suspected terrorists
- FOUR –

Girl:  Organizations!?  Like what?

Officer ONE:  Hmm… (flips pages) Greenpeace, food not bombs, students to end all war, people for the ethical treatment of animals, (insinuating) earth first!  the drug reform coordination network!                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              

Girl:  Those aren’t terrorist!  Those are for peace and justice for people everywhere and our environment!

Officer Two: Not according to this.

Officer ONE:  (continues)
- FOUR: visiting of websites that, taken together, are strongly correlative of a pattern of terrorist activities, including (flips pages) Erowid drug vaults, the nation dot org, peace now dot org, indymedia dot org, and save Afghanistan dot net.
But then!

Officer Two: Then!

Officer ONE:  Then, you took the action that brought down the authorization: you sent a letter or package containing an anthrax-related threat to the President of the United States!

Girl:  What!! That was a joke!  Oh my God!  We never sent anthrax!

Officer ONE:  No?

Girl:  It was a tape with Anthrax on it!

Officer ONE:   You did send real Anthrax!?  (officers look at each other)

Officer Two: Where did you get it? 

Officer ONE:  Who taught you how to do this stuff?  (to Officer Two) Our tests for the germ warfare agent were negative!

Officer Two:  Yet she admitted under your questioning!

Officer ONE:   That’s why we do this, Denkins.  That’s part of how we keep the Homeland secure!

Girl:  Germ warfare!?  You morons!  It was a tape of the band Anthrax.

Officer ONE:   You’re goddamn right Anthrax is banned!  Banned and restricted by the department of Home –

Girl:  No, the rock music band!  The tape had music from this group called Anthrax!  It was a joke!  We sent music to the President!

Officer ONE:   You sent heavy metal music to the President of the United States!

Officer Two: My God!

Officer ONE:   That’s disgusting!  The sick and twisted youth of this Godless time!

Officer Two: Thank God we found out!

Officer ONE:   Cuff her, Denkins!  I’ll call for backup!
